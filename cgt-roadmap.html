05 — Data Strategy

The moat is not the data.
It's what sits on top.

The data layer is commoditising. Competitive advantage lives in the reconciliation logic and audit trail — built in three phases, each scoped to what's actually licensable, not scraped.

P1
Mid-market APIs + manual override
Historical pricing and basic corporate action data from accessible APIs. Manual workflow for AMMA statements and ATO class ruling references. Accept incomplete automation — make manual entry fast and auditable.
IEX Cloud Morningstar Manual AMMA
P2
AU registry integration — verification layer
Pursue a registry data relationship (Automic Group, Computershare, Link) for what a registry actually holds: current holding balances and the mechanics of a corporate action — ratio, record date, DRP price — as the issuer's own registrar. This confirms the parcel register; it isn't a cost-base source. Registries don't carry original purchase price, so P3 stays the source of truth for cost base.
Automic Group Computershare Link Market Services
P3
Direct broker connectivity
API feeds from high-volume AU brokers to automate transaction ingestion. Reduces data entry burden and error rates — the primary accounting firm value proposition. Start with the top 4 by retail volume.
CommSec nabtrade Selfwealth Stake
Scoped against what's licensable, not scraped — the shared data-warehouse idea I considered, and ruled out
The instinct. Collect corporate-action and pricing data once — off ASX announcements, or a company's own site — and reuse it across every client, instead of paying per call. An expanding shared warehouse that gets more valuable the more firms query it.
Why it doesn't survive contact with the terms. ASX's own Terms of Use bar exactly this: Market Announcements are for personal/private use only, and any commercial use needs ASX's written authority — enforced with active crawler-blocking and law-enforcement referral, not a dormant clause. IRESS, Refinitiv/LSEG and Morningstar sit on the same structure: redistribution is a separately-licensed, separately-priced right, not something that follows from having access. Industry norm, not an ASX quirk.
What the incumbents do instead. Sharesight and BGL/Class don't scrape — they pay for it: an ASX delayed-pricing partnership, direct broker integrations, and registry "Data Packs" sold as a commercial product. The one piece that isn't just a paid API — AMIT/AMMA tax-component detail — comes from bilateral relationships fund managers build over years. That's a partnerships moat, not a technical one, and it's realistic to plan for only once there's client volume to justify those conversations.
What this means for the phasing above. P3's broker feeds stay the primary, cost-base-bearing source — it's the client's own data, effectively free as part of the brokerage relationship. P2's registry layer is a balance-and-mechanics check, not a replacement. One more legitimate channel worth planning for: the ATO's own Annual Investment Income Report feeds its prefill service for registered tax agents through the same SBR channel this product already assumes for pushing the CGT figure back — a per-client, consent-gated pull, which is also why this stays a per-firm data strategy rather than a shared warehouse across firms.
Not legal advice — directional product research to scope the build, checked against each vendor's own published terms.
06 — Success Definition

What year one
actually looks like

Three layers of measurement — correctness, adoption, and retention signal. Revenue is a year-three metric.

Layer 01 — Correctness
Zero ATO-disputed outputs from the parcel engine. In a compliance product, defect rate matters more than feature count.
100% cost base conservation verified on all BSP and BONUS corporate action events. Conservation failure is a product defect, not a rounding issue.
All 14 origin types producing correct acquisition dates, cost bases, and CGT discount eligibility assessments in acceptance testing.
Layer 02 — Adoption
50 accounting practices actively using the tool by end of year one — each with at least 10 client portfolios tracked.
Average 30+ parcels per portfolio suggests users are entering real, complex data — not testing with toy portfolios.
Net Promoter Score > 45 among accounting firm principals — the referral channel is the fastest path to growth in this segment.
Layer 03 — Retention signal
60%+ of firms process at least one corporate action through the tool rather than manually. This signals the workflow is embedded, not just sampled.
Tax-season return rate: % of firms who actively use the tool during the June–October CGT reporting window. This is the real retention test.
Reconstruction requests: number of clients where the tool is used to reconstruct 5+ years of historical parcels — a leading indicator of deep dependency.
What we do NOT measure in year one
Revenue from enterprise or SMSF lodgement volume — those are year-three metrics and require regulatory credentialing we haven't built yet.
Feature count or sprint velocity — in a compliance product, shipping correct features slowly beats shipping wrong features fast.
Market share vs BGL or Class Super — they're serving different segments. Year one is about owning the mid-tier beachhead, not fighting the enterprise market.
07 — Go-to-Market

Two viable paths.
One clear recommendation.

Discovery settled the buyer question: the accounting firm is the buyer, not the retail investor (who is DIY and price-sensitive). The SMSF enterprise segment is closed to new entrants for 5–7 years; the mid-tier firm path is achievable in 18–24 months.

★ Recommended
Path A — Firm-first, freemium as wedge
Sell directly to small–mid accounting practices — the validated buyer — on the capabilities they can't get elsewhere: correct corporate actions, AMMA processing, multi-client triage, and an ATO-ready audit trail. Run a free retail tier purely as a lead-gen and data wedge — satisfied investors refer their accountant (the Pro buyer) — not as the core motion. Revenue lands in year one, and the moat is compliance depth, not consumer network effects.
Validated buyer Year-one revenue
Alternative
Path B — Embedded API model
Build the CGT parcel engine as a licensed B2B API — the GainsKeeper model. License to accounting software vendors, super admin platforms, and broker-dealer tools. Avoids the distribution and trust problem entirely by becoming infrastructure rather than a competing product. Faster revenue, but no direct user relationship.
Faster B2B revenue No consumer moat
