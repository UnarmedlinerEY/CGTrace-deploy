CGTRACE

One product, traced from a messy market to a defensible bet.

This is not a pitch deck. It's a behind-the-stage peek at how a PM thinks — the same product followed end to end, from raw market noise through to a costed roadmap. Each chapter is a working artifact; together they show the reasoning that connects them. Open them in order, or jump to any one.

The through-line: one insight — that the unmet job is cost-base reconciliation inside the accountant's workflow, not another portfolio tracker — earned in discovery and carried, unbroken, into design, pricing, and strategy.

What the whole portfolio demonstrates

A through-line, not a folder of deliverables

Evidence → decision

Every downstream choice — the wedge, the pricing, the roadmap — traces to a dated, persona-tagged quote, not an assumption.

One insight, carried

The cost-base reconciliation wedge appears in discovery and reappears as the spine of the design and the anchor of the pricing. Coherence is the point.

Judgment under ambiguity

Where the artifacts disagreed, I reconciled them to the evidence rather than presenting a contradiction — and said so out loud.

The honest edge: this is a conditional go, not a victory lap. The pain is validated; whether firms will pay for authoritative-data integration is a commercial question still open — the next step is 6–8 firm interviews, not more desk research. A portfolio that names its own risk is more credible than one that doesn't.
